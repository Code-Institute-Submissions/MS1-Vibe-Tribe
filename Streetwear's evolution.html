What is streetwear?


Street culture is a term used to refer to urban culture. Like all cultures, streetculutre has an asthetic of its own. Streetwear is casual wear that has become popularise by its exclusive nature. The time line below will document the evolution of this asthetic and give insight into the different factions of street culture that have impacted the asthetic. Read more

Hip-Hop

Hip-Hop started in the 70s and as it gained popularity, people began to embrace the new stye of fashion that was created alongside it. By the 90s Hip-Hop had popularised ‘street-wear’ moving it from a subsector of fashion to a mainstream trend. During the 90s Hip-Hop icons like Tupac & TLC can be seen wearing barnds such as Dickies, Timberlands, Tommy Hilfiger & Karl Karni. This was the begining of designer brands catering to the ‘street culture’ asthetic. Read More.


Skating

Whilst Hip-Hop is said to have birthed street culture. Skate culture was also a source of influence. ‘Street sports’ such as skating had become increasingly popular by the 80s with brands Like Stussy catering specialifically to skate culture. They both have played a crucial role in devloping the 90s asthetic. Even now many rappers and skaters share a similar asthetic, decades later. With Converse being a popular brand amongst skaters and basketball players alike at that time. Today Nike has more domminance on both scenes.Read More.


Japenese Streetwear

Japanese streetwear also known as Harajuku aesthetic is very prevelant in global streetwear culture. In the 70s and 80s Japanese youth began to rebel against the elders traditions and embrace western culture. Japanese streetwear is a representation of this defiance and non conformitism. These themes are still present in streetwear today, making it a key form of expression amongst creatives, globally. Many of the youth moved to the Harajuku district of Japan in order to escape the traditionalist society, this birthed creativty and designing in Japan as we know it today. Brands such as UNDERCOVER, NEIGHBORHOOD & WTAPS paved the way for brands such as Bape, Commes Des Garcons & AMBUSH.Read More

To this day the Jordan and Nike are mayor players in the sneakers game. Sports attire such as jerseys and sneakers have had great influnce on fashion. Some of which is still evident today.
